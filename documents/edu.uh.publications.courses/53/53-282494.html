BCHS 6335 - Cellular Neuroscience

Credit Hours: 3.00
Lecture Contact Hours: 3.0 Lab Contact Hours: 0.0
Prerequisite: BIOL 1306, BIOL 1307, and BIOL 4315.

Advanced study of central nervous system with the emphasis on cellular, network, and system functions. The course is subdivided into four main sections: 1) the excitatory and inhibitory cell and network anatomy and function and excitatory-inhibitory plasticity and pathological imbalance; 2) experimental invasive and clinical non-invasive functional imaging techniques; 3) canonical and emerging neurotransmitter systems, with the emphasis on the serotonergic and endocannabinoid signaling; 4) in depth analysis of the neuropathology and therapeutic approaches to epilepsy, migraines, Alzheimerâ€™s disease, and neurological sequel of COVID-19 infections.
