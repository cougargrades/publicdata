PCOL 6270 - Emerging Technologies for Cancer Drug Discovery and Development

Credit Hours: 2
Lecture Contact Hours: 2 Lab Contact Hours: 0
Prerequisite: Students should have a thorough knowledge of biology, biochemistry, cell biology and chemistry.

This course is for graduate students interested in learning about emerging technologies for cancer drug discovery and development. In addition, many of the topics covered in this course are applicable to the broader context of drug discovery. The course will cover a variety of disciplines and topics important to cancer drug discovery and development. The course will start by covering pharmacology and basic cancer biology. Then the course will transition to introductions to assay design, lead compound identification, medicinal chemistry and pharmaceutics. Finally, preclinical animal models and clinical assessments will be presented.
