CORR 6330 - Protective Coatings and Inhibitors

Credit Hours: 3
Lecture Contact Hours: 3 Lab Contact Hours: 0
Prerequisite: CORR 6360 Materials and Corrosion OR equivalent course.

Corequisite: None.

This course provides an overview of coating fundamentals and reasons for coating along with various types of coatings. The course focuses on curing mechanisms, mixing and thinning and methods on the application equipment. It defines the primary function of a coating and states the purpose of each coating component and identifies factors that impact the selection of coatings. The practice of coating selection, repair and performance will be highlighted. The students will also learn how to select and utilize various types of corrosion inhibitors for different systems.
