SOC 3338 - Punishment in the U.S.

Credit Hours: 3.0
Lecture Contact Hours: 3.0 Lab Contact Hours: 0.0
Prerequisite: SOCI 1301 or consent of instructor.
Description
An in-depth analysis of the nature, functions (and dysfunctions), and causes of punishment within the U.S.; the role of race, gender, poverty, the media, corporations, and state agents; different forms of punishment and conceptions of personal responsibility and subjectivity; the consequences of capital punishment, both at the micro- and macro-level.
Repeatability: No

Additional Fee: No
